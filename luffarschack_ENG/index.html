Five in a Row in a partially observable environment

In regular five-in-a-row, both players see the whole board. But what happens if parts of the board are hidden?

01
Hidden cells – Cross-hatched cells are hidden. You can place your mark there, but you won't see what's underneath.
02
Blind moves – If you play on a hidden cell, you won't know whether the move succeeded. If the cell was already taken by your opponent, the move is lost — you lose your turn without having placed anything.
03
Five in a row wins – Same rules as usual. Whoever places 5 in a row first wins.

This is called partial observability — the agent only sees part of the environment's state. How does that affect your ability to make good decisions?
