This mini-model and ChatGPT are built on the same basic principle – predicting the next word. The difference is how the probabilities are worked out.

Similarities
• Both predict the next word from the words that came before.
• Both compute a probability for many possible next words and then pick one of them.
• Temperature affects the word choice the same way in both models.

Differences
• This model uses a table of exact word sequences. ChatGPT uses a neural network with billions of parameters.
• ChatGPT can take thousands of previous words into account, not just 1–3.
• ChatGPT was trained on an enormous amount of text from many different sources.
• ChatGPT can recognise similar patterns and generalise. This model can only use word sequences it has seen before.
